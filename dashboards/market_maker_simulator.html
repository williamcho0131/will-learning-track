🎓 Welcome, Future Market Maker!

Your Mission: Make markets, capture spread, manage risk.

What is Market Making? You simultaneously offer to buy (bid) and sell (ask) an asset. You profit from the spread between your bid and ask prices.

Key Concepts:

  • Spread: Difference between your ask and bid (your profit margin)
  • Inventory: Your net position (long = inventory, short = negative inventory)
  • Adverse Selection: When informed traders pick off your quotes
  • Market Regimes: Calm (tight spreads), Volatile (wide spreads), Trending (one-sided flow)

Your Goal: Keep inventory near zero, capture spread, avoid being run over by informed traders.

Current Lesson: Start by placing your bid and ask quotes around the mid price.

• If someone hits your bid (sells to you), you go LONG

• If someone lifts your ask (buys from you), you go SHORT

• Your profit is the spread between where you buy and sell

• WARNING: If price moves against your inventory, you lose!
