## 📈 TRADING CONCEPT: Market Microstructure — The Bid-Ask Spread is Lying to You

**What you probably know:** The spread is the gap between highest bid and lowest ask.

**What you need to master:** The spread is a *signal*, not just a cost.

### The Three-Layer Model:

```
LAYER 1: Quoted Spread
  ↳ What you see on the screen (bid/ask)
  
LAYER 2: Effective Spread  
  ↳ Where your order actually fills vs mid-price
  
LAYER 3: Realized Spread
  ↳ The spread after the next trade (did you get picked off?)
```

### The "Adverse Selection" Problem:
When you hit a bid that's "too good to be true," chances are:
- A HFT model saw something you didn't (news, flow, correlated market)
- You're trading against someone with better information
- You just became someone's exit liquidity

### Practical Application:
**Before entering ANY trade, ask:**
1. Is the spread tight (<0.1%) or wide (>0.5%)?
2. Has volume spiked in the last 5 minutes? (Flow incoming)
3. Is the L2 depth symmetrical, or is one side thin? (Slippage risk)
4. Are correlated assets (BTC/ETH, SPY/QQQ) moving together or diverging?
